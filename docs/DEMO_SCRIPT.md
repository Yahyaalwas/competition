## Presenter Tips

- **Speak to policymakers, not engineers.** Use "decision-makers", "ministers", "policymakers" — not "users"
- **Let the data speak.** Pause after showing the AI report. Let judges read it.
- **Quantify impact.** "2.5 percentage points" and "rank improves from 4th to 2nd" are more memorable than "the model improved"
- **Emphasise bilingual.** Click the Arabic tab deliberately and pause — many judges have never seen this in an AI competition submission
- **Own the limitations.** Mentioning limitations ("this is heuristic elasticity, not econometric estimation") builds more credibility than pretending perfection
- **End with the human story.** "60 million people" is the right note to close on
